Panathinaikos Aktor January Analysis

EuroLeague: A Data-Driven Deep Dive

Project Analysis: January – February Deep Dive

This project examines the performance of Panathinaikos in the EuroLeague, covering all matches from January 6, 2026, to February 3, 2026. Our goal is to dissect every possession to uncover the tactical trends of the Greek team.

This analysis is based on original data collected by watching every game of the period one by one. The project focuses on non-traditional statistics to determine if these data points can yield significant insights about a team's performance. To simplify the analysis, the metrics are split into offensive and defensive categories, providing a clear view of how the team operates in each phase of the game.

🏀 Offensive End Metrics

Detailed tracking of scoring efficiency and shot creation:

  • Offense Second: Seconds taken to execute the offense.
  • Time Zone: The specific "Time Zone" of each shot attempt (3 different zones: Fastbreak/Transition, Set Game, Late Clock).
  • C/U: Contested, slightly contested, or uncontested attempts.
  • 2p/3p: 2-pointers vs. 3-pointers.
  • Made/Missed: Shooting results (Made/Missed/Foul).
  • Off. Reb. After Miss: Offensive rebounds secured.
  • Who Took It (O.R.): Identifying the responsible player for the rebound.
  • Shot Created: Method of shot creation (8 distinct categories).
  • Player Of Interest: Key involved players.

🛡️ Defensive End Metrics

Analysis of defensive schemes and effectiveness:

  • Screen Defense: Type of defense applied on opponent screens (5 different types of defenses).
  • Player: Player defending screen.
  • Basket Allowed: Tracking allowed shots vs. not allowed ones.
  • No Shot Result: Turnover, steals, fouls, etc.
  • C/U: Contest levels on allowed shots.
  • 2p/3p: 2-pointers vs. 3-pointers.
  • Made/Missed: Shooting results (Made/Missed/Foul).

Key Performance Insights: Winning vs Losing Patterns

  • Late Clock Execution: Higher volume of late-possession shots during losses leads to increased inefficiency, despite appearing uncontested. Opposing teams do not allow Olympiakos to run the floor easily, and make easy baskets from fastbreaks and transitions. This indicates forced decision-making from the team, because of the shot clock pressure.
  • Transition Game: A noticeable decrease in fastbreak opportunities during losses suggests a failure to capitalize on defensive stops and control the game rhythm. The team will have to base their game in more 5 vs 5 possesions.
  • Shot Creation Consistency: Tactical adherence remains high across outcomes, with very small difference between games won and games lost. This is a sign of a team with its own identity, that follows coach's game plan in each game. Wether it is beneficial for the reasons, that have been previously mentioned, or not, won't be decided in this project because the game sample is not sufficient enough.
  • 3rd Quarter Imbalance: Over-reliance on 3-point shots after halftime, coupled with poor accuracy, often results in losing momentum and significant point swings. It is also confirmed just by observing each game's box score, that in some 3rd quarters Olympiakos has received more points on defense, than points scored on offense.
  • Offensive Rebound Utilization: While the volume of offensive rebounds is consistent through wins and losses, the efficiency of "second-chance" points drops significantly in losses, which translates to a significant percentage of those second chances being wasted.
  • Pick 'n Roll Defense: Opponent accuracy increases by 15% during losses, primarily due to lapses in screen coverage and defensive rotations.
