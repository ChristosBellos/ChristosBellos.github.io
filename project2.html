Panathinaikos Aktor January Analysis

EuroLeague: A Data-Driven Deep Dive

Project Analysis: January – February Deep Dive

Just like in Project 1, this project examines the performance of Panathinaikos in the EuroLeague, covering all matches from January 6, 2026, to February 3, 2026. Our goal is to dissect every possession to uncover the tactical trends of the Greek team and find any possible patterns that will help preventing it from negative results.

This analysis is based on original data collected by watching every game of the period one by one. The project focuses on non-traditional statistics to determine if these data points can yield significant insights about a team's performance. To simplify the analysis, the metrics are split into offensive and defensive categories, providing a clear view of how the team operates in each phase of the game.

🏀 Offensive End Metrics

Detailed tracking of scoring efficiency and shot creation:

  • Offense Second: Seconds taken to execute the offense.
  • Time Zone: The specific "Time Zone" of each shot attempt (3 different zones: Fastbreak/Transition, Set Game, Late Clock).
  • C/U: Contested, slightly contested, or uncontested attempts.
  • 2p/3p: 2-pointers vs. 3-pointers.
  • Made/Missed: Shooting results (Made/Missed/Foul).
  • Off. Reb. After Miss: Offensive rebounds secured.
  • Who Took It (O.R.): Identifying the responsible player for the rebound.
  • Shot Created: Method of shot creation (8 distinct categories).
  • Player Of Interest: Key involved players.

🛡️ Defensive End Metrics

Analysis of defensive schemes and effectiveness:

  • Screen Defense: Type of defense applied on opponent screens (5 different types of defenses).
  • Player: Player defending screen.
  • Basket Allowed: Tracking allowed shots vs. not allowed ones.
  • No Shot Result: Turnover, steals, fouls, etc.
  • C/U: Contest levels on allowed shots.
  • 2p/3p: 2-pointers vs. 3-pointers.
  • Made/Missed: Shooting results (Made/Missed/Foul).

Panathinaikos Aktor

During this time period, Panathinaikos played, in both Greek League and Euroleague, a total of 11 games. The team managed to win 7 of them and lost 4. During those matches some interesting stats were collected, that deserve some notice.

Panathinaikos' average time for each game, before a shot was taken, was approximately 13.58 seconds. Specifically, a discrimination between the team's wins and losses is appplied.

The difference between each time zone's uncontested shots is perfectly clear on the chart above. Although during set game and late clock situations the uncontested shots percentage is almost the same, if not bigger, the significant difference is made during fastbreak situations.

For every 10 fastbreak/transition shots the team takes in games won, almost 6 of them will be uncontested, whereas in lost games less than 3 shots will be uncontested. In that major part of Panathinaikos' game style, which is to run the floor, opponents apply more pressure to fastbreak shots in order to take away an important tool that will lead the team to a winning result.

In games won out of the total uncontested shots taken, almost 21% came from fastbreak/transition situations (2 out of 10 uncontested shots), and 71.5% came from set game situations (more than 7 out of 10 uncontested shots). The least percentage amount comes from late clock situations with a percentage of 7.8% (less than 1 out of 10 uncontested shots).

In games lost the metric switches between fastbreaks and late clock situations. Out of all uncontested shots taken almost 7% comes from fastbreaks/transitions (less than 1 out of 10 uncontested shots) and almost 20% comes from late clock situations (2 out of 10 uncontested shots). The percentage of set game situations is almost the same as the one in games won.

This means that Panathinaikos has more uncontested options during fastbreaks in games won, which is a result of opponent's turnovers or missed shots that led to quick offenses. In games lost the teams has less easy options during fastbreaks, which is a result of 1. less fastbreaks perfromed and 2. more pressure from the opponent.
